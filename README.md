## The Part Most Coupon Articles Skip: Understanding ScraperAPI Credits

Before you pick a plan, you need to understand how credits actually work. This is the single most important thing about ScraperAPI's pricing — and it's buried in the documentation.

**The headline: 1 credit ≠ 1 page scrape.** The number of credits a request costs depends on what type of site you're scraping and what features you enable. Here's the full credit multiplier table:

| Request Type | Credits Per Request |
|---|---|
| Standard request (plain HTML) | 1 |
| `render=true` (JavaScript rendering) | 10 |
| `screenshot=true` | 10 |
| `premium=true` (premium proxy) | 10 |
| `premium=true` + `render=true` combined | **25** |
| `ultra_premium=true` | 30 |
| `ultra_premium=true` + `render=true` combined | **75** |
| Cloudflare/Datadome/PerimeterX bypass (auto) | 10 |

And domain-specific costs are applied automatically — you don't control these:

| Target Domain | Credits Per Request |
|---|---|
| Standard websites | 1 |
| Amazon (e-commerce) | 5 |
| Google / Bing SERPs | 25 |
| LinkedIn | 30 |

Notice that combining `premium=true` and `render=true` costs **25 credits**, not 20 (10+10). And `ultra_premium=true` with `render=true` costs **75 credits** — nearly double what you'd naively expect from adding them separately. This non-linear stacking is the biggest source of surprise credit burn.

**Real-world example:** If you're on the Hobby plan ($49/month, 100,000 credits) and you're scraping JS-heavy pages on Amazon with premium proxy enabled:
- Amazon: 5 credits base
- Premium + render: 25 credits
- **Total: 30 credits per request**
- **Effective requests from "100,000 credits": ~3,333 product pages**

That's not 100,000 pages. It's 3,333. Understanding this math before you pick a plan (and before you apply your coupon) will save you from a billing surprise mid-month.
